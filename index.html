About Me

Hello, I'm Sharon McDermott! I am originally from the sunny shores of Santa Monica, California; I ventured to the vibrant city of Las Vegas at 10 years old, which I've since called home. My journey into data science began with a profound desire to help others, which initially led me to pursue a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology from the University of Nevada, Las Vegas. There, I discovered the transformative power of understanding human behavior and the potential to influence positive change through insightful analysis. This deepening interest in research and data and my practical experiences in various professional settings propelled me to pursue a Master of Science in Data Science.

Beyond my academic and professional life, I am an avid storyteller, a hobby that fills my home with notebooks brimming with narratives. This passion for storytelling transcends into my professional work in data science. I leverage narrative techniques to transform complex data sets into compelling, understandable stories that inform and drive strategic decisions. I approach each challenge with a blend of analytical rigor and creative flair.

I focus on solving problems and crafting data narratives that elucidate the why and how of policy impacts and organizational improvements to make informative decisions. By integrating my background in psychology with my skills in data science, I aim to foster environments where data informs and inspires action.

Philosphy

Welcome! As a Data Science graduate with a specialty in data management and analytics. I am dedicated to enhancing program efficiency and effectiveness within public sectors. My philosophy is rooted in using a utilitarian approach, focusing on maximizing community well-being through strategic data use. This ethical philosophy guides my efforts toward transparency and integrity, aiming to deliver informed, ensuring compassionate data-driven decisions to enhance program efficiency and overall societal good. Data science is more than just a profession to me—it's a responsibility and critical instrument in modern governance and healthcare. Utilizing meticulous research and strategic analytics, I work to uncover patterns that lead to innovative policies and interventions, aiming to enhance individual and community lives.

Experience

As a former Forensic Specialist at the Nevada Department of Health and Human Services, I managed a blend of security and client care within a secure mental health facility, overseeing the custody and treatment of mentally ill offenders. This role required rigorous law enforcement duties alongside direct healthcare support, ensuring adherence to legal mandates, and assisting in patient rehabilitation. My career transitioned into healthcare research where, as a Research Coordinator at Cleveland Clinic and Retina Consultants of Nevada, I managed complex research protocols and collaborated with principal investigators. This involved patient screening, data collection, and specimen processing, significantly boosting my expertise in data system management and clinical data integrity. Subsequently, at the National University, I furthered my role in academia as a Research Assistant, supporting grant writing and project management while applying research integrity and effectiveness. These cumulative experiences underpinned my dedication to data, integrity, and innovation in improving healthcare outcomes and academic research efficacy.
